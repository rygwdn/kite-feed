B.C. Highway 3 reopens with detour traffic controls

Category: Canada

A key stretch of Highway 3 in British Columbia has reopened to traffic between Hope and Princeton after heavy rain and atmospheric rivers washed out part of the roadway earlier this month [1][2][3][4]. The reopening came on Christmas Day, earlier than provincial officials initially expected, and it is operating as single-lane alternating traffic on a temporary detour [1][2]. The washout removed about 50 metres of road near 42 kilometres east of the Highway 3/5 junction, prompting the closure that disrupted a major Lower Mainland-to-Interior route [1][2].

Primary Source: View original article

Did you know: The Pattullo Bridge replacement project is budgeted at about $1.6 billion, and it recently opened a northbound lane on the new stal̕əw̓asəm (Riverview) Bridge [1].

Key Points

  • Work pace: The transportation ministry credited round-the-clock contractor work and readily available materials for the ahead-of-schedule reopening [1].
  • Driving rules: The temporary detour is posted at 30 km/h, with traffic control and reduced speeds in place [1].
  • Avalanche safety: Westbound drivers may be held about 500 metres back to prevent queuing in an avalanche-risk zone where stopping is not allowed [1].
  • Trucking guidance: Commercial vehicles are being urged to use alternate routes until the detour can support two-way traffic, which officials expect in the new year [1].
  • Regional detours: Drivers can also travel between the Lower Mainland and the Interior via the Coquihalla Highway, though it saw closures and delays earlier this week due to heavy snow and crashes [1].

Perspectives

B.C. government (Transportation and Infrastructure): Officials framed the reopening as a holiday-timed restoration of connectivity after the Dec. 16 washout, advising drivers to expect traffic control through the repaired area.
