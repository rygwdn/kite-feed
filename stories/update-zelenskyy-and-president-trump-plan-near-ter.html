Update: Zelenskyy and President Trump plan near-term meeting

Category: World

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said he and President Donald Trump have agreed to meet “in the near future,” after Zelenskyy held talks with U.S. negotiators about ending Russia’s war in Ukraine [7][10][14]. Zelenskyy suggested decisions could come quickly, saying “a lot can be decided” before the New Year, as Washington intensifies diplomacy while Moscow and Kyiv maintain sharply different demands [1][2]. The diplomatic activity is unfolding amid continued fighting, including Russian strikes described by one report as more than 100 missiles launched overnight into Ukraine [3].

Primary Source: View original article

We have agreed on a meeting at the highest level — with President Trump in the near future. A lot can be decided before the New Year. Glory to Ukraine!

— Volodymyr Zelenskyy (POLITICO Europe)

Did you know: The draft proposal discussed in the reporting includes a potential special economic zone concept for parts of Donbas, linking economic incentives to a possible diplomatic settlement [18].

Key Points

  • Peace proposal: Politico reports that talks have centered on an updated “20-point” draft plan that Kyiv says has been shared with Moscow for feedback [18][3].
  • Donbas economics: The latest draft includes the possibility of creating a special economic zone in parts of Donbas, the eastern region claimed by Moscow [18].
  • Security guarantees: Zelenskyy says the updated plan keeps proposed security guarantees from the U.S., NATO and European partners that he describes as equivalent to NATO’s Article 5 protections [18].
  • Where talks happened: Ukraine’s negotiator Rustem Umerov met U.S. lead negotiator Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner in Miami last week, following meetings in Berlin involving Ukrainian and European officials [18].
  • Russia’s signal: Russia, in separate comments cited by France 24, described the peace efforts with Washington as moving “slow but steady,” while saying it is analyzing the proposal [5][18].

Perspectives

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy: He says recent contacts with the U.S. side were productive and that a top-level meeting with President Trump could help bring “real peace” closer on an accelerated timeline [18].

Sources: POLITICO Europe

U.S. negotiators (Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner): Zelenskyy has described their Christmas Day discussion as focused on “substantive details” and has portrayed it as a constructive exchange aimed at shaping a possible deal framework [8].

Sources: CBS News

Russian side: Russian statements cited in reporting suggest Moscow sees gradual progress in discussions with Washington and says it is reviewing the latest proposal [5][18].

Sources: France 24

Context from reporting on the talks: Coverage notes that President Trump’s diplomatic push faces “sharply conflicting demands” from Moscow and Kyiv, underscoring the main obstacle to a quick agreement [2].

Sources: The Hindu

Timeline

  • Christmas Day: Zelenskyy holds what he calls a “really good” conversation with U.S. envoy [3].
  • Thursday: Zelenskyy says he spoke about ending the war with Witkoff and Kushner [11][8].
  • Friday: Zelenskyy says he and President Trump agreed to meet soon [7][10].

Technical Details

  • NATO Article 5: Article 5 is NATO’s collective-defense clause, treating an attack on one member as an attack on all; Zelenskyy says the plan’s proposed guarantees would be equivalent in effect [18][common].
  • Special economic zone: A special economic zone typically offers tailored rules or incentives to attract investment; the draft plan raises this option for some areas of Donbas [18][common].
  • Draft “points” framework: The reporting describes a multi-point draft proposal (including an updated “20-point” version) circulating among parties as a structured basis for negotiations [18][3].

Historical Background

Russia launched a full-scale invasion of Ukraine in February 2022, triggering Europe’s largest war in decades and sustained Western military and financial support for Kyiv [common]. Multiple rounds of talks over the years have struggled over core issues such as territorial control and long-term security arrangements for Ukraine [common]. The current flurry of diplomacy comes as the war approaches its fourth year, according to the reporting [3][17].

Q&A

Q: What elements of the updated proposal remain unacceptable to either side, according to officials?

A: The coverage notes that the U.S. push has run into “sharply conflicting demands” from Moscow and Kyiv, but the articles provided do not list each side’s full set of red lines in detail [2].

Q: Will European governments be directly involved in any security-guarantee mechanism discussed in the plan?

A: One account says Zelenskyy expects guarantees involving the U.S., NATO and European partners, but the reporting provided does not specify which European countries would take on which commitments [18].

Q: Where is the Zelenskyy–Trump meeting expected to take place?

A: The articles say the meeting is planned “in the near future,” but they do not definitively confirm the location [7][10][14].
