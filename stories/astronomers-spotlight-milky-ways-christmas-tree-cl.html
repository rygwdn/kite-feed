Astronomers spotlight Milky Way’s Christmas Tree Cluster

Category: Science

Astronomers are highlighting NGC 2264, a festive-looking star-forming region about 2,700 light-years from Earth that resembles a glowing holiday tree [1][2]. The region includes the Christmas Tree Cluster of young stars, with the Cone Nebula near the top and the Fox Fur Nebula below, creating a recognizable “tree” shape in astronomical images [1]. Spanning nearly 80 light-years, the scene offers a vivid example of how newborn stars illuminate and sculpt surrounding clouds of gas and dust—an uplifting reminder that our galaxy is still actively building new suns [1][2].
