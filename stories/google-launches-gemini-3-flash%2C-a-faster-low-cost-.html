Google launches Gemini 3 Flash, a faster low-cost AI

Category: AI

Google has introduced Gemini 3 Flash, a new large language model designed to deliver fast responses and strong reasoning at significantly lower cost than its premium Gemini 3 Pro model, while matching or surpassing the older Gemini 2.5 Pro on many benchmarks [deepmind.google#1][testingcatalog.com#1][simonwillison.net#1]. The model supports long-context multimodal inputs including text, images, audio, video, and PDFs, and is already rolling out across the Gemini app, Google Search’s AI mode, and Google Cloud services such as the Gemini API, Vertex AI, and Gemini Enterprise so it can serve both everyday users and developers at scale [testingcatalog.com#1][blog.google#1][blog.google#2][google.com#3]. Pricing starts at about $0.50 per million input tokens and $3 per million output tokens, which Google and external observers note is roughly a quarter of Gemini 3 Pro’s price at shorter context lengths and even cheaper at longer contexts, making advanced AI more accessible for experimentation and production use [testingcatalog.com#1][simonwillison.net#1][smol.ai#1]. Early benchmark results shared by Google and independent trackers show Gemini 3 Flash performing competitively with larger frontier models in coding, tool-using "agentic" tasks, and academic reasoning evaluations, with the company positioning it as a practical default for many workloads where latency and budget matter [testingcatalog.com#1][smol.ai#1][techcrunch.com#1].

Key Points

  • Everyday integration: Gemini 3 Flash is becoming the default model in the consumer Gemini app and the on‑device assistant backing Google Search’s AI mode, bringing its faster responses to millions of users without manual configuration [blog.google#1][google.com#3][techcrunch.com#1].
  • Developer availability: Developers can access Gemini 3 Flash immediately through the Gemini API, Google AI Studio, Vertex AI, Gemini Enterprise, the Gemini CLI, and Android Studio, allowing rapid integration into apps, backends, and coding workflows [testingcatalog.com#1][blog.google#2].
  • Adaptive efficiency: The model dynamically adjusts its internal compute based on task complexity and uses around 30% fewer tokens on typical everyday requests compared with earlier setups, which helps keep both latency and costs down for frequent usage [testingcatalog.com#1][deepmind.google#1].
  • Coding performance: On the SWE-bench Verified coding benchmark, Gemini 3 Flash achieves about 78% accuracy, which external commentators note as strong enough for many production coding assistants and automated software maintenance tools [testingcatalog.com#1][smol.ai#1].
  • Long-context design: Gemini 3 Flash offers a context window of roughly one million input tokens and up to tens of thousands of output tokens, enabling use cases like analyzing large codebases or lengthy documents in a single run without chunking [testingcatalog.com#1][simonwillison.net#1].

Technical Details

  • Thinking level options: Gemini 3 Flash exposes four "thinking" modes—minimal, low, medium, and high—that adjust how much internal compute the model uses, letting developers trade off latency and cost for more deliberate reasoning without switching models [simonwillison.net#1].
  • Tool calling and agents: The model is optimized for tool calling, with demos showing it coordinating up to 100 external tools, which enables more capable agent-style workflows that can browse, call APIs, and manipulate data programmatically [smol.ai#1][blog.google#2].
  • Context window and modalities: Gemini 3 Flash accepts up to around one million tokens of mixed text plus images, audio, video, and PDFs but responds in text only, making it suitable for analyzing large multimodal inputs like full code repositories or long reports [simonwillison.net#1][testingcatalog.com#1].
