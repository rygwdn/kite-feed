Toronto police probe second mezuzah thefts this month

Category: Canada

Toronto police are investigating after mezuzahs were removed from outside Jewish homes/condo units in North York in a second incident reported this month [1][5][4][3]. Police were called just before 12:30 p.m., and the service’s hate crime unit was notified as the investigation continues [1][4]. The incident has raised concerns in the local Jewish community, while authorities work to determine whether the thefts meet the threshold of a hate-motivated offence [4][3].

Primary Source: View original article

Did you know: Many Jewish households place a mezuzah on the right side of the doorway as a long-standing religious tradition [common].

Key Points

  • Units affected: Police said mezuzahs were taken from the doorways of three condo units in North York [5].
  • Pattern flagged: Multiple reports describe this as the second similar mezuzah-removal incident reported in Toronto this month [4][3][5].
  • Hate-crime review: Toronto police indicated their hate crime unit has been notified as part of the response to the reported thefts [4].
  • Community concern: National Post characterized the thefts as a potential hate-crime issue affecting Jewish residents whose mezuzahs were taken from their doorways [3].
