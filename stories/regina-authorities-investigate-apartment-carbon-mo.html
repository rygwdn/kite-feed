Regina authorities investigate apartment carbon monoxide death

Category: Canada

Regina officials are investigating a carbon monoxide incident at a multi-family building on the 1800 block of Albert Street after first responders found two residents unconscious, with one pronounced dead at the scene and the other taken to hospital in critical condition [1][3]. The City of Regina said crews detected “dangerous levels of carbon monoxide,” shut down the source with gas inspectors, and later cleared impacted residents to return home [1]. Separate reporting identified the person who died as 11-year-old Henry Losco, while his father, Sergio Losco, survived and remained in hospital [2][4]. Community members have organized financial support for the family through an online fundraiser [3].

Primary Source: View original article

Did you know: The City of Regina noted carbon monoxide is colourless and odourless, making working detectors especially important for early warning [1].

Key Points

  • Work underway: CBC reported the carbon monoxide spread through the building while maintenance work was being done [2].
  • Multi-agency probe: The City of Regina said the investigation involves Regina Police Service, the city fire department, the Technical Safety Authority of Saskatchewan, and the Saskatchewan Coroners Service [1].
  • Family remembrance: From hospital, Sergio Losco described his son as “brilliant and kind” and said the 11-year-old loved soccer [4].
  • Community support: A GoFundMe was launched to help the family following the carbon monoxide death, reflecting an outpouring of local support [3].
  • Detector guidance: The City of Regina reminded residents that homes with fuel-burning appliances or attached garages need carbon monoxide detectors, placed outside sleeping areas and on every level with a fuel-burning appliance, and that alarms should never be ignored [1].
