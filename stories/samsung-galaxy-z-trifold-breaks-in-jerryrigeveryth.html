Samsung Galaxy Z TriFold breaks in JerryRigEverything test

Category: Technology

Multiple outlets report that Samsung’s first tri-folding phone, the Galaxy Z TriFold, failed an extreme durability/torture test published by YouTuber Zack Nelson (JerryRigEverything) [4][2][5][1]. The coverage emphasizes that the tri-fold design introduces additional hinges and folding sections, creating more potential failure points than a typical bi-fold device [1][5]. In the test, the handset bent and ultimately broke, with reports noting the display stopped working after the damage [5][1]. Despite the dramatic result, some articles stress the scenario is an intentionally extreme “torture test” and not representative of ordinary daily use [5][2].

Key Points

  • Limited availability: The Galaxy Z TriFold is currently only available in Korea, with other regions (including the US) still waiting for broader release, according to Android Police’s coverage [2].
  • Audience scale: Digital Trends notes Zack Nelson framed the test as a Christmas upload to his 9.7 million YouTube subscribers, highlighting how quickly durability impressions can spread [4].
  • Foldable comparison: Several write-ups compare the tri-fold’s outcome to Samsung’s newer bi-fold Galaxy Z Fold 7, describing the bi-fold as better able to withstand bend abuse than the tri-fold [1][5].
  • Failure mode: SamMobile reports that during the torture test the device bent and broke and that its screen “died,” underscoring how display functionality can be the practical end-point for foldable damage [5].
  • Design tradeoff: 9to5Google argues the tri-fold form factor can be harder to ruggedize simply because “there’s just more to break,” pointing to additional mechanical complexity as a durability challenge [1].

Technical Details

  • Tri-fold hinge system: A tri-fold phone relies on multiple hinges and fold lines to create a larger tablet-like surface, and the added mechanical complexity can introduce more potential failure points under bending forces [1][5].
  • Torture testing: Durability “torture tests” intentionally apply stresses beyond normal daily use to reveal weak points quickly; SamMobile and Android Police both describe this TriFold video in that extreme-testing context [5][2].
  • Display failure as endpoint: Reports describe the screen failing after the device bent/broke, which matters because a foldable can look intact externally yet become unusable if the flexible display or its connections are damaged [5][1].
