Quebec Liberal leader Rodriguez resigns amid fundraising probe

Category: Canada

Quebec Liberal Party Leader Pablo Rodriguez has resigned after weeks of internal turmoil and emerging allegations about irregular fundraising practices during the leadership race he won in June, which is now the subject of a criminal investigation by the province’s anti‑corruption police [thestar.com#1][thestar.com#2][nationalobserver.com#1]. Reports said Montreal wine importer Emanuel Cabral allegedly hosted a spring event at his home to support Rodriguez, where some guests were reportedly given envelopes with $500 in cash that they were encouraged to donate to the campaign, a practice banned under Quebec’s political financing rules because donations must come directly from an individual and cannot be reimbursed [montrealgazette.com#1]. The controversy triggered a revolt inside the Liberal caucus and mounting public pressure, leading Rodriguez to step down and opening the door to another leadership race for the struggling party [lapresse.ca#2][thestar.com#1].
