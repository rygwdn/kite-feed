Xiaomi launches 17 Ultra with 200MP zoom ring

Category: Technology

Xiaomi has unveiled the Xiaomi 17 Ultra in China as a new premium flagship phone positioned around high-end mobile photography, including a Leica-backed variant [1][3][5]. Across reports, the phone’s headline camera hardware includes a large 50MP 1-inch main sensor and a 200MP periscope telephoto, with some versions adding a DSLR-like mechanical/physical zoom ring to make zooming feel more like a dedicated camera [3][2][4]. The launch also highlights top-tier performance parts such as Qualcomm’s Snapdragon 8 Elite and a very large 6,800mAh battery, aiming to combine photography-focused design with day-to-day endurance [1]. Xiaomi framed the Leica partnership as a way to improve low-light imaging and overall “mobile imaging” capability as it competes with other ultra-premium phones [5].

Primary Source: View original article

We work together to build future-proof optical technology … and completely reshape the mobile photography experience,

— Lu Weibing (South China Morning Post)

Did you know: Android Headlines says the 17 Ultra’s 50MP ultrawide camera includes autofocus and a 115° field of view [4].

Key Points

  • Display durability: Android Headlines says the phone uses a 6.9-inch LTPO AMOLED panel (120Hz) that can reach 3,500 nits, plus Dragon Crystal Glass 3.0 and IP66/IP68/IP69 ratings for dust and water resistance [4].
  • Continuous optics: The 200MP periscope system is described as offering continuous optical zoom from 75mm to 100mm via mechanical movement, aiming to avoid digital cropping across those focal lengths [4].
  • Sensor tech: The 50MP 1-inch main camera uses Xiaomi’s Light Fusion 1050L sensor with LOFIC, which Android Headlines says improves dynamic range in high-contrast scenes [4].
  • Price positioning: TechMeme’s summary of GSMArena reports a starting price of $999+ for the 17 Ultra in China, placing it firmly in the ultra-premium tier [3].
  • Comparative demo: SCMP reports Xiaomi showed side-by-side dim-light photo comparisons in Hong Kong during the launch framing, spotlighting night photography as a key upgrade area [5].

Technical Details

  • Periscope telephoto: A periscope lens folds the light path inside the phone to enable longer focal lengths; here it’s paired with a 200MP sensor and (in some versions) a mechanical zoom ring for tactile control [2][3].
  • LOFIC dynamic range: Android Headlines says the 50MP 1-inch main sensor uses LOFIC (Lateral Overflow Integration Capacitor) to better handle high-contrast scenes by improving dynamic range [4].
  • Continuous optical zoom: Android Headlines describes a mechanically moving lens that provides continuous optical zoom from 75mm to 100mm, keeping “native resolution” across that range rather than relying on digital cropping [4].

Industry Impact

  • Mobile photography: A mechanical zoom ring and continuous optical zoom could nudge the industry toward more tactile camera controls on phones, potentially influencing accessory makers and competing flagships to prioritize ergonomics, not only sensor counts [2][4].
  • Component ecosystem: The combination of a 1-inch main sensor and a 200MP periscope highlights ongoing demand for large imaging sensors and compact moving-lens modules, which can shape supplier roadmaps for sensors and miniaturized optics [3][4].
  • Durability benchmarks: If IP66/IP68/IP69 protection becomes more common at the high end, it may raise consumer expectations for water/dust resistance on camera-centric phones that include moving parts [4].

Historical Background

Smartphone makers have increasingly competed on camera performance—especially larger sensors, periscope telephoto designs, and computational photography—as phones replaced many casual cameras for everyday use [common]. Xiaomi’s partnership with Leica reflects a broader industry pattern of phone brands teaming with established camera names to differentiate image processing and optics in the premium tier [1][5].
