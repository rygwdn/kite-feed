YouTube secures exclusive global streaming rights for Oscars

Category: Technology

The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences has signed a multi‑year agreement making YouTube the exclusive global home of the Oscars starting with the 101st ceremony in 2029, ending the awards show’s decades‑long run on Disney-owned ABC after the 100th ceremony in 2028 [engadget.com#1][fastcompany.com#1][betanews.com#1]. Under the deal, the main telecast, red carpet, Governors Awards and other events will stream live and free worldwide on YouTube, while also being available to YouTube TV subscribers in the US [engadget.com#1][fastcompany.com#1][betanews.com#1]. The Academy says the move is designed to expand accessibility for its growing international audience through features like closed captioning and multiple audio languages, and to consolidate year‑round programming such as the nominations announcement, student awards and educational content on the Oscars YouTube channel [slashdot.org#1][engadget.com#1][fastcompany.com#1]. The partnership also includes collaboration with Google Arts & Culture to digitize parts of the Academy’s museum collection, further blending Hollywood’s heritage with modern online platforms [engadget.com#1][mashable.com#1].

We are thrilled to enter into a multifaceted global partnership with YouTube to be the future home of the Oscars and our year-round Academy programming

— Bill Kramer and Lynette Howell Taylor (Engadget)
