OpenAI launches ‘Your Year with ChatGPT’ recap

Category: Technology

OpenAI has rolled out “Your Year with ChatGPT,” a Spotify Wrapped-style year-end recap that turns a user’s 2025 ChatGPT activity into a shareable slideshow with personalized highlights [2][4][7][8]. The recap surfaces a snapshot of how people used the chatbot—mixing usage stats with playful, AI-generated creative elements—aimed at helping users reflect on their year with the tool [9][3][10]. Availability is tied to specific account and settings requirements, and early reporting notes that rollout and eligibility vary by region and plan type [3][7][6].

Primary Source: View original article

Did you know: Some users’ recaps include tongue-in-cheek superlatives (similar to Spotify Wrapped “awards”) based on how they used ChatGPT during the year [9].

Key Points

  • What it measures: The recap can include details such as your busiest chat day, a description of your “chat style,” image-generation activity, and even a count of “em-dashes exchanged,” presented in a slideshow format [7][9][2].
  • Creative extras: Several outlets report it also adds AI-generated flourishes like a custom poem and pixel-art or themed visuals based on topics you discussed, encouraging easy saving and sharing [9][3][10].
  • Privacy switches: Access depends on having “reference saved memories” and “reference chat history” enabled, and conversations in Temporary Chat mode are excluded, which means privacy-focused users may need to change settings to see a recap [3][7][10].
  • Who gets it: Reports differ on geographic availability, with coverage noting rollout to users in the US, UK, Canada, New Zealand, and Australia, and that it is available in English to Free, Plus, and Pro users while excluding Business/Enterprise/Edu (and some organizational accounts) [3][7][6][10].
  • How to trigger: If it doesn’t appear automatically, guides say users can update the app, look for the feature on the home screen, or manually prompt it (including by adding/asking for “Your Year with ChatGPT”) to try to surface the recap [4][7][9].

Perspectives

OpenAI (feature rollout): OpenAI positions the recap as a fun, shareable “Wrapped”-style look back that summarizes a user’s year of ChatGPT usage, including stats and personalized elements [7][2][4].

Sources: PCWorld

Privacy-conscious users and observers: Some coverage highlights that eligibility hinges on enabling memory and chat-history settings, prompting discussion about the tradeoff between personalized features and minimizing retained data [10].

Sources: eWEEK

International users awaiting access: Outlets note the feature is not yet available everywhere, with expectations that OpenAI may expand access beyond the initial set of countries over time [3].

Sources: Android Headlines

Timeline

  • December 22, 2025: OpenAI announces rollout of “Your Year with ChatGPT” [7][3].
  • December 23, 2025: More outlets publish guides for finding and using recaps [5][4].

Technical Details

  • Reference saved memories: A ChatGPT setting that lets the system retain user-specific “memories” to personalize responses; it must be enabled for the yearly recap to work in reported rollouts [3][10].
  • Reference chat history: A setting that allows past chats to be used for continuity and personalization; it is also required to generate the year-end recap in coverage [3][7][10].
  • Temporary Chat mode: A chat mode whose conversations are excluded from the recap, meaning those sessions won’t show up in the year-end statistics [10].

Industry Impact

  • Consumer AI engagement: By packaging usage into shareable graphics and “awards,” the recap mirrors social platforms’ viral loops, potentially pushing more user-generated posts about AI tools and normalizing AI as an everyday companion product [10][9].
  • Privacy-by-design pressure: Because the recap depends on memory and chat-history settings, it spotlights how feature design can incentivize data retention—likely increasing demand across the industry for clearer opt-ins, explanations, and parity for privacy-preserving modes [10][7].
  • Enterprise segmentation: Excluding Business/Enterprise/Edu (and some org accounts) suggests AI vendors may keep “fun” consumer engagement features separate from compliance-oriented enterprise offerings, reinforcing a two-track product strategy in the sector [6][10].

Historical Background

Year-end “Wrapped”-style recaps became a common product pattern after music and video platforms popularized annual summaries designed for sharing on social media [common]. As generative AI tools became mainstream for work, creativity, and everyday questions, AI companies have increasingly added engagement features that help users understand and showcase how they use these systems [10].

Q&A

Q: Do Team or workplace accounts get the same recap experience as personal accounts?

A: Coverage says the feature is not available to Business, Enterprise, or Edu plans, and reporting also notes exclusions for some organizational account types [6][10].

Q: Will my recap include chats from Temporary Chat mode?

A: No—reporting notes Temporary Chat conversations are excluded from the year-end recap [10].

Q: What kinds of “awards” might the recap assign, beyond basic stats?

A: Articles describe behavior-based superlatives that vary by usage patterns, alongside archetype-style categorization in some recaps [9][3][10].

Action Items

  • Find your recap from the ChatGPT home screen: Open ChatGPT on web or update the iOS/Android app, then look for “Your Year with ChatGPT” promoted on the homepage to start the slideshow [2][9].
  • Try a manual trigger if it’s missing: If you don’t see it, follow troubleshooting guides that suggest prompting ChatGPT (including asking to “show me my year with ChatGPT”) or using the in-app add/plus flow where available [4][7].
  • Check data settings before enabling sharing: Review whether “reference saved memories” and “reference chat history” are on (and note Temporary Chat exclusions) so you understand what the recap can and can’t include before you generate or share it [3][10].
