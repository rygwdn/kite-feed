Amazon in talks to invest over $10bn in OpenAI

Category: AI

OpenAI is in early-stage discussions for Amazon to provide at least $10 billion in new funding in a deal that would also see the AI company run some of its workloads on Amazon’s in-house AI chips instead of relying solely on Microsoft’s Azure cloud [ft.com#1][techcrunch.com#1][google.com#1]. The potential investment could lift OpenAI’s valuation above $500 billion and would deepen its strategy of pairing large cash infusions with long-term cloud and hardware commitments from big tech partners [ft.com#1]. Reports describe the structure as similar to existing arrangements between OpenAI and Microsoft, where capital, computing infrastructure and AI models are tightly linked in multi-year contracts [ft.com#1][techcrunch.com#1]. None of the companies have announced a final agreement, and the talks could still change as details are negotiated [ft.com#1][techcrunch.com#1].

Did you know: Some modern AI funding deals are structured so that a cloud provider’s investment is smaller than the long-term minimum spend the AI company commits back on that same provider’s infrastructure, effectively creating a loop of capital and compute [techcrunch.com#1].

Key Points

  • Chip partnership: The discussions include OpenAI adopting Amazon’s Trainium and Inferentia accelerator chips for some AI training and inference workloads, potentially expanding beyond its current heavy dependence on Nvidia-based infrastructure and Microsoft’s Azure data centers [ft.com#1][techcrunch.com#1][google.com#1].
  • Strategic diversification: By engaging Amazon alongside Microsoft, OpenAI would gain a second major cloud and hardware partner, reducing single-vendor exposure and giving it leverage in pricing and access to scarce AI compute resources [ft.com#1][techcrunch.com#1].
  • Circular deal trend: The proposed structure follows a growing pattern in AI where enterprise customers receive capital from cloud providers in exchange for committing to spend far larger sums on those same providers’ infrastructure over time [techcrunch.com#1].
  • Valuation dynamics: A funding round of this scale would place OpenAI’s implied worth above the market capitalizations of many established software companies, underscoring how investors now value foundation model providers as core digital infrastructure rather than just application vendors [ft.com#1].
  • Competitive landscape: An Amazon–OpenAI arrangement would intensify competition with Google and other cloud platforms, which are simultaneously developing their own large models and courting AI startups with preferential access to compute and cash incentives [techcrunch.com#1][google.com#1].
