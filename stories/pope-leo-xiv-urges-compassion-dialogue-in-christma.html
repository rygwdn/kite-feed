Pope Leo XIV urges compassion, dialogue in Christmas address

Category: World

Pope Leo XIV used his first Christmas homily and “Urbi et Orbi” blessing at the Vatican on Thursday to spotlight civilian suffering in Gaza and to call on people not to “shed indifference” when others have lost everything [2][3][5][11]. He also appealed for peace and dialogue in multiple conflicts, including urging Russia and Ukraine to pursue talks to end the war [7][12][18]. The message was delivered to a crowd reported at about 26,000 in St. Peter’s Square and was closely watched as a first major Christmas statement of his pontificate, with several outlets noting comparisons to his predecessor’s style and priorities [5][9].

Primary Source: View original article

Did you know: “Urbi et Orbi” is Latin for “To the City and to the World” [5].

Key Points

  • Multilingual greetings: Leo revived a tradition by offering Christmas greetings in multiple languages during his first Christmas Day message [13][6].
  • Focus on migrants: In additions to war zones, he highlighted migrants crossing the Mediterranean Sea and the American continent in search of a better future as part of the suffering he wanted listeners to notice [5][15].
  • ‘Forgotten’ wars: He singled out victims of “forgotten” wars and named conflicts in parts of Africa, including Sudan, as he broadened his appeal beyond the most-covered fronts [10].
  • Christmas Eve theme: At Midnight Mass on Christmas Eve, Leo urged care for the poor and for migrants, framing charity as a concrete response to hardship [17][4].
  • Justice and stability: He called for “justice, peace and stability” in Lebanon, the Palestinian territories, Israel and Syria, while also praying for the “tormented people of Ukraine” [16].

Perspectives

Pope Leo XIV: He urged believers to reject indifference and respond with compassion, while calling for peace and dialogue in conflict zones including Gaza and the war between Russia and Ukraine.

Technical Details

  • Urbi et Orbi blessing: A formal papal address and blessing traditionally delivered at Christmas to the city of Rome and the wider world, used by Leo to summarize major global crises [5][common].
  • Christmas homily vs. address: Reports distinguish between Leo’s Christmas homily at Mass and his later Christmas Day “Urbi et Orbi” message, which broadened his appeals across multiple conflicts and humanitarian issues [2][5].

Historical Background

“Urbi et Orbi” (“To the City and to the World”) is a traditional papal Christmas blessing delivered from St. Peter’s Basilica to Catholics worldwide [common]. Recent popes have often used the occasion to list major global conflicts and humanitarian crises as a call to prayer and diplomacy [common]. Multiple reports noted that Leo’s first Christmas message was watched closely because it was his first as pontiff and invited comparison with Pope Francis’ approach to Christmas appeals [9].

Q&A

Q: Which conflicts did Leo mention beyond Gaza and Ukraine?

A: Reports say he referenced Yemen, Lebanon, Syria and Sudan, alongside concern for migrants and other war victims [15][16][10].

Q: Why did some coverage call the Christmas appeal unusually direct?

A: Outlets described his Gaza remarks as a more direct intervention than typical for a solemn Christmas liturgy, which is often primarily spiritual in tone [2][1].
