Brazil ex-president Bolsonaro has hernia surgery, backs son

Category: World

Brazil’s jailed former president Jair Bolsonaro underwent a scheduled double-hernia operation in Brasília that doctors described as successful, and they expect him to remain hospitalised for several days [1][3][4][5][8]. A Supreme Court justice authorised a temporary release from prison for the procedure, with police ordered to remain outside his hospital room and restrictions reported on electronic devices [6]. During his hospital stay, Bolsonaro also formally endorsed his son, Senator Flávio Bolsonaro, as a candidate for Brazil’s 2026 presidential race [2][6][7].

  • Painful condition: The surgery addressed a painful double hernia, and one medical estimate put the expected hospital stay at about five to seven days [4].
  • Sentence context: Bolsonaro has been serving a 27-year prison sentence since November linked to an attempted coup after Brazil’s 2022 election [8][3][1].
  • Hospital security: Court-authorised conditions around the hospital stay included police stationed outside the room and reports that computers and mobile phones were prohibited [6].
  • Family political bid: Flávio Bolsonaro is identified in reports as a 44-year-old senator, and his father’s endorsement was issued while recovering in hospital [6].

In this latest episode, a judge allowed him to leave prison temporarily for surgery, underscoring how courts can grant limited medical releases even for high-profile inmates under sentence [6].
