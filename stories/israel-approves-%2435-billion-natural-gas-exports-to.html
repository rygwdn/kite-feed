Israel approves $35 billion natural gas exports to Egypt

Category: World

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has approved an export permit for a long‑term agreement to supply natural gas from Israel’s offshore Leviathan field to Egypt, in what he called the largest gas deal in the country’s history, valued at roughly $35 billion over 15 years [nytimes.com#1][ft.com#1][apnews.com#1][al-monitor.com#1]. The gas will be produced by a consortium led by US energy company Chevron and Israeli partners and shipped by pipeline to Egypt, which already imports Israeli gas for its domestic market and liquefied natural gas (LNG) export plants on the Mediterranean [thehindu.com#1][nytimes.com#1][al-monitor.com#1]. Netanyahu said the agreement will generate tens of billions of shekels in revenue for Israel’s state budget and bolster spending on services such as education, health, infrastructure and security, while strengthening Israel’s position as a regional energy supplier [newarab.com#1][timesofisrael.com#1]. The deal moved forward after months of delay and discussions with Washington, and some Israeli civil society groups have urged the government to publish more details, including implications for domestic gas reserves and household prices [ft.com#1][newarab.com#1].

Today, I approved the largest gas deal in Israel's history.

— Benjamin Netanyahu (The New Arab)

Did you know: Gas typically supplies around 70 percent of Israel’s electricity generation, making decisions about export volumes especially important for its domestic power security [nytimes.com#1].
