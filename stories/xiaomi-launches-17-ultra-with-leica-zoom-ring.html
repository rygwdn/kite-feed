Xiaomi launches 17 Ultra with Leica zoom ring

Category: Technology

Xiaomi has launched the Xiaomi 17 Ultra in China, positioning it as a top-end flagship focused on mobile imaging, with both a standard model and a Leica-branded special edition [1][2][3][5]. Across reports, the headline photography hardware includes a 50MP 1-inch main camera and a 200MP periscope telephoto, aimed at stronger zoom and low-light performance [2][4][3][5]. The Leica Edition adds a tactile, DSLR-like mechanical zoom ring that can wake the camera and be remapped to other manual controls, a nod to making smartphone shooting feel more hands-on and fun [2][3][6]. Pricing cited for China starts around $995+ (as aggregated by Techmeme/GSMArena), underscoring that this is firmly in the premium tier [4].

Primary Source: View original article

We work together to build future-proof optical technology … and completely reshape the mobile photography experience,

— Lu Weibing (South China Morning Post)

Did you know: Engadget says the zoom ring can detect displacements as small as 0.03mm, which is unusually precise for a consumer phone control [2].

Key Points

  • Display and toughness: Android Headlines says the phone uses a 6.9-inch LTPO AMOLED panel (2,608×1,200) with adaptive 120Hz, up to 3,500 nits peak brightness, Dragon Crystal Glass 3.0, plus IP66/IP68/IP69 ratings for dust and water resistance [3].
  • Battery and charging: Engadget reports a 6,800mAh silicon-carbon battery with 90W wired and 50W wireless charging, aimed at keeping heavy camera use from feeling like a trade-off [2].
  • Mechanical zoom range: Android Headlines describes a continuous optical zoom mechanism spanning roughly 75mm to 100mm on the 200MP periscope, using mechanical movement to avoid relying on digital cropping across that range [3].
  • Leica styling extras: Engadget notes Leica Edition touches like a two-tone finish, a Leica-style red dot, textured edges, film simulations (including Monopan 50), and a “Leica Moments” 3:2 shooting mode; it also ships with accessories like a lens cap, lanyard, magnetic case and cleaning cloth [2].
  • Imaging comparison pitch: SCMP highlights Xiaomi’s marketing push that the Leica-backed imaging upgrades improve night photography and can be compared side-by-side against Apple’s iPhone Pro Max in dim scenes, with Xiaomi president Lu Weibing framing the partnership as “future-proof optical technology” [5].

Technical Details

  • LOFIC image sensor tech: Android Headlines says the main camera sensor integrates LOFIC (Lateral Overflow Integration Capacitor) to improve dynamic range, which helps preserve highlights and shadows in high-contrast scenes [3].
  • Manual zoom ring: Engadget describes the Leica Edition’s mechanical ring as a physical control that can launch the camera when rotated, detect tiny movement (down to 0.03mm), and be reassigned to settings like exposure compensation or manual focus [2].
  • Continuous optical zoom module: Android Headlines reports the periscope telephoto uses mechanical movement to provide continuous optical zoom between about 75mm and 100mm while keeping native resolution rather than switching to digital cropping in that band [3].

Industry Impact

  • Mobile camera design: The mechanical zoom ring and continuous optical zoom mechanism suggest a broader industry push toward physical, camera-like controls as phone makers look for new ways to differentiate imaging beyond software processing [2][3][6].
  • Accessory ecosystems: Bundling items like a lens cap, lanyard, case and cleaning cloth turns a phone into a camera-style kit, potentially opening more room for official accessories and third-party add-ons tied to camera use [2].
  • Premium competition: SCMP frames the 17 Ultra’s imaging pitch as a direct challenge to Apple’s iPhone Pro Max positioning in photography, highlighting how camera performance remains a key battleground at the high end [5].

Historical Background

Xiaomi and Leica have collaborated on smartphone imaging for multiple product generations, and the 17 Ultra continues that co-branding as a way to differentiate camera hardware and tuning in a crowded premium market [1][5]. Ultra-tier smartphones have increasingly competed on camera systems (bigger sensors, longer telephoto reach, computational photography), as brands try to replace more everyday camera use cases with a device people already carry [common].

Q&A

Q: What storage and RAM options are offered at launch?

A: Engadget reports configurations up to 16GB LPDDR5X RAM and up to 1TB of UFS 4.1 storage [2].

Q: What other cameras are included besides the main and periscope telephoto?

A: Engadget says the lineup also includes a 50MP ultrawide and a 50MP selfie camera [2].

Q: What’s unique about connectivity or security on the Leica Edition?

A: Engadget reports the Leica Edition includes a special encryption chip and a dual-satellite connection feature [2].

Action Items

  • See whether the zoom ring matches your shooting style: If you get hands-on time with the Leica Edition, try rotating the ring to wake the camera and then remap it to exposure compensation or manual focus to see which control feels most natural [2].
  • Stress-test night photos in your own scenes: Recreate a consistent dim-light shot (same spot, same framing) and compare outputs across the main camera and telephoto to evaluate the low-light improvements Xiaomi highlights [5].
