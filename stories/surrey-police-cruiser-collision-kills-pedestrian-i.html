Surrey police cruiser collision kills pedestrian; IIO probes

Category: Canada

A female pedestrian died after a collision with a Surrey Police Service vehicle in the Whalley area of Surrey, B.C., early Christmas morning [1][2]. Police said the crash happened around 6 a.m. and involved a police vehicle as it was leaving a gas station [2]. The Independent Investigations Office of B.C. has been notified and is investigating the circumstances of the death [1][2].

Primary Source: View original article

Did you know: In British Columbia, the Independent Investigations Office is a civilian agency that investigates incidents of death or serious harm involving on-duty police officers [common].

Key Points

  • Scene details: Police tape cordoned off part of a Chevron station at 108 Avenue and 132 Street, where evidence markers and clothing were visible on the ground [1].
  • Hospital outcome: Surrey police said the pedestrian suffered life-threatening injuries and later died in hospital [1].
  • Watchdog role: The IIO confirmed investigators were sent to the scene but said it would not release additional details at that time [1].
  • Recent pattern: Vancouver Sun reported this was the second fatal pedestrian collision this month involving a Surrey police cruiser, following another death reported on Dec. 14 at 152nd Street and 64th Avenue [1].
  • Next updates: Surrey Police Service said it will provide more information soon [1].
